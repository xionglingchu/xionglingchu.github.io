Part I

1. What is the Order？

All peoples throughout history have had systems or orders for governing how they deal with each other. I call the systems within countries “internal orders,” those between countries “external orders,” and those that apply to the whole world “world orders.” These orders affect each other and are always changing. Such orders have always existed at every level— within families, companies, cities, states, and countries, as well as internationally.

这让我想起来了2021年在美团打车做经营分析时，为了验证单城跑通下的最小临界值规模，leader提出了正循环模型。当时这个思路很好，其实那阵说的应该属于internal order。最近听说这个模型在2023其他team要重新搞，那我觉得这次是不是应该把其他competitors和整个ecosystem考虑进去，考虑external order。

3. Behind the cycles: Determinants

3.1.The 8 determinants behind the rising and declining of world order

  • rising education leads to increased innovation and technology

  • tech leads to an increased share of world trade and military strength, stronger economic output, the building of the world’s leading financial center

  • with a lag, the establishment of the currency as a reserve currency

3.2 Three Phases a country exprience

  • Rising Phase

    tech –> more competitive in product $ more economic output –> more share in world trade –> financial center –> ppl want to save the currency)

  • At the top

    as people get used to doing well, they increasingly bet on the good times continuing—and borrow money to do that—which leads to financial bubbles.

  • The decline

    The decline phase typically comes from internal economic weakness together with internal fighting, or from costly external fighting, or both.

    Debt becomes large => cannot borrow more from others => could only print more money => inflation =>

    Debt becomes large => cannot borrow more from others => rise tax on rich man => rich man move to other country and take away assets => economy down => poor man get poorer, the gap between the rich and the poor enlarge => internal conflict escalates => civil war

e.g. WW2
