The great talk between Elon and Lex_Dec21

发表于2022-07-27|更新于2022-10-30|Tool
|字数总计:1.4k|阅读时长:8分钟|阅读量:

要理解tesla earning call number;

你需要理解tesla;

要理解tesla，你需要理解elon和他的思考方式。

太多网文/公众号的各种分析只停在第二层，比如提到特斯拉的4680电池，提到特斯拉的一体化压铸，减少了xx%的零部件。但是更值得学习和膜拜的是，Elon是怎么想到这些的，他的思考逻辑有何高明之处，以及掌握基本的工具——大学物理学知识。

今天北京下了一天雨，坐在窗边，空气很清晰，伴着雨声，听elon娓娓道来他的做事观和方法体系，我想，这定是心流体验了！下面，把听到的很有enlightened的地方记录下来：

  1. Prototype is easy, manufacturing is hard.

  2. Why does the Engine scale manufacturing is hard to you？

    Becasuse there are a lot of components and unique materials.

  3. how long we could achieve can be estimated by physics.

  1. When facing difficulty, quitting is not my nature. I dont care about optimism or pessimism. I just get it down.

  2. Can you introspect your thinking process just like your often spoken 1st principle?

    Physics is the law. So fisrt make sure you dont violate physics such as the energy and momentum. As for the 1st principle, it can be applied in any walks of life really. So you should first find the basic physic foundation, which is the axiomatic truth, then reason up from the truth.

    Another physics tool is putting the things in the limit. You scale a particular in a very large number or to a very small number, and think how does things change. Manufacturing is a good example.

    E.g. 1) If a product is expensive, is it because our volume is low? or sth fundamentally foolish that we are doing?
    So, you could check it by thinking what if our volume was a million units a year? Is it still expensive? If the volume is not the issue, there’s something fundamental about the design.

    E.g. 2) If you are good at manufacturing, you can make the cost of the product asymptotically approach the raw material value. It is hard to do, but it is possible for anything.

  1. Get the cost of tesla bot or whatever complex systems that are traditionally seens expensive down. What mostly often happen during the product design is that ppl try to use their familiar tools and methods to do it. And try to create a product using exsiting tool. The another better way to think about it is to try to imagine the planotic idea of a perfect product, whatever it might be. So what is the perfect arrangement of the atoms? And then try to figure out how to get the atoms in that shape.
  1. Ppl easily fall victim to the tradition especially the way things are done in the past. It’s the function of inertia. ppl want to use the same tool and methods that they are familiar with. That’s what they will do by default. The perfect product can be our moving target. You can successfully approximate a more perfect product.

  1. autopilot

    Lex: Tesla autopilot has been through an incredible journey over the past 6 years.

    Elon: Yes, i think that’s where we first connected really.

    Lex: I know how difficult it is and have a lot of colleagues and fridends. So there was a natural skepticism. So when the first time i got into it, i think there is no way this car could maintain. So my intuition initially was lane-keeping problems is too difficult to solve.

    Elon: Oh, lane-keeping, that’s relatively easy!

    Lex: But solve in the way just we talked about previous prototype versus a thing that actually creates a pleasant experience over hundreds of thousands of miles.

    Elon: We have to wrap a lot code around the mobileye thing. It doesn’t just work by itself.

    Lex: yes. I mean that’s part of the story of how you approach things sometimes. Sometimes you do things from scratch. Sometimes at first you kind of saw there and then you decide to scratch from it. That was one of the boldest decision I’ve seen is both on the hardware and software to decide to eventually go from scratch. I thought, again, and i was skeptical of whether that’s going to be able to work out, cause it’s such a difficult problem.

    first is the imagenet model of computer vision, like what’s in academia, this is like the real world artificial intelligence.

    ELon: We have a lot of talented people. And Tesla autopilot team is extremely talented. It is like some of the smartest people in the world. So we are getting it doen.

    Lex: So I am aware that there is a incredible team. What are some insights you’ve gained over those 5 6 years of autopilot about the problem of autonomous driving.

    Elon: When it comes to those days, it is to solve self driving, you have to solve. You also need to recreate what human do to drive, which is human’s drive with optical senses, eyes and biological neural nets. So actually for full self driving to work, we have to recreate that in digital forms. So we have to that means cameras with a banch of neural networks in silicon form. That’s the only way, i don’t think there’s any other way.

    Lex: But the question is what aspects of human natures do you have to encode into the machine, right? So you have to solve the perception problem, like detect and you further and you first realize what is the perception problem for driving like all the things you have to able to see. like what we do we even look at when we drive. There’s I just recently heaed Andrej talked about MIT, about like car doors. I think it was the world’s greateast talk of all time about car doors. you know, the fine details of cardoors, like what is even an open car door,man, so like the ontology of that, that’s perception problem we human solve that perception problem and tesla solve that problem. and there’s the control and planning coupled with perception. It’s figured out like what’s involved in driving, especially all the difficult edge cases. And I mean, maybe you can comment on this. how much game theoretic kind stuff needs to be involved. you know at four-way stop sign. You know as humans, drive, our actions affect the world. It changes how others behave. Most autonomous driving you are usually just responding to the scene. As opposed to like really asserting yourself in the scene. Do you think?

    Elon: I think these sort of control logic conundrums are not hard problems.

    Lex: What do you think is the hard part? In the whole beautiful complex problem?

    Elon: It’s a lot of freaking software man and a lot of smart lines of code. For sure, in order to have create an accurate vector space. So like you’re coming from image space, which like this flow of photons going to camera and then since you have this massive bitstream in image space. And then you have to effectively compress the massive bitstream conrresponding to photons that knocked off an electron in like a camera sensor and turn that bitstream into a vector space. By vector space, i mean like, you got cars and humans and lanelines curves traffic lights and that kind of thing. Once you have a accurate vector space, the control problem is similar to that of a video game like “Grand Theft Auto” or “Cyberpunk”. If you have accurate vector space, the control prolem is, I wouldn’t say it’s trival, it’s not trival, but it’s not like some insurmountable thing.
